So this is it — the initial commit. dply is officially a thing now, even if "a thing" today mostly means a Laravel app with a lot of empty rooms and a hopeful folder structure.

The pitch in my head is simple: a Forge-style platform for managing servers and sites, but one I actually enjoy using. That's the whole north star. Everything else is downstream of that.

The first commit is big — 452 files, which sounds like a lot until you remember most of it is framework boilerplate, vendored UI components, and the bones of a few features I couldn't resist sketching before I'd even pushed once. There's a Models layer roughed in, some Services, a pile of Jobs, the first migrations, and enough Livewire UI to render something that isn't a stack trace. There are even a few tests, which I'd like to claim is discipline but is really just nerves.

## what's actually here

- The model + migration skeleton for servers and sites
- A first pass at the Livewire workspace shell and some UI components
- Services and Jobs stubs for the work that'll eventually run over SSH
- Just enough tests to prove the wiring isn't haunted

Nothing here is impressive yet. It's a foundation, and foundations are boring on purpose. The interesting decisions — how deploys flow, how the workspace feels, how much I can hide vs. expose — all of that is ahead of me.

Building in public from commit number one feels a little vulnerable, but that's sort of the point. If this works, you'll get to watch it grow up. If it doesn't, well, you'll get to watch that too.

Tomorrow: start turning the skeleton into something with a pulse.
