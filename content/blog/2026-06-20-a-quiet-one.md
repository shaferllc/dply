Not every day is a 78-commit marathon, and honestly thank goodness. After the week's module surgery, today was a single small commit — sixteen files, mostly in the Cloud module and a couple of site workspace views.

I won't pretend it was a grand plan. After a big refactor there's always a tail of little things you notice when you're actually clicking around the app instead of staring at the file tree: a view that reads slightly wrong now that its engine moved, a Cloud surface that needed a small nudge to line up with the rest. So I did that and left it there.

There's a discipline in stopping when the meaningful work for the day is done rather than inventing busywork to feel productive. The Cloud product is the one place I'm most careful about — it's meant to be the seamless, app-first surface where you never see the container backend underneath — so even small touches there get more thought than the file count suggests.

Short post for a short day. Back to it tomorrow.
